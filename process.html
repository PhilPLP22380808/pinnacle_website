Frequently Asked Questions

Got questions? We've got answers.

How long does conveyancing take?
A typical transaction takes 8-12 weeks from instruction to completion, though this can vary depending on chain length, search turnaround times, and how quickly all parties respond. We work to keep things moving as fast as possible.
What's the difference between exchange and completion?
Exchange is when contracts become legally binding — both parties are committed. Completion is when money changes hands and you get the keys. There's usually 1-2 weeks between exchange and completion, though it can be on the same day.
What are searches and why do I need them?
Searches reveal important information about the property that isn't visible on a viewing — things like planning applications, flood risk, contaminated land, and local authority matters. Most mortgage lenders require them, and we recommend them even for cash buyers.
Who will be my point of contact?
Once we've reviewed your case, we'll pair you with a dedicated lawyer. You'll always have two named contacts — so if one is unavailable, the other can help immediately. Same-day responses are our standard.
