How It Works

From instruction to keys in hand — here's what to expect at each stage of your conveyancing journey.

1

Agree a Transaction

Your offer is accepted on a purchase, your property is sold subject to contract, or both. Once a transaction is agreed, you can instruct your conveyancer to get started straight away.

Offer accepted Sale agreed Chain confirmed
2

Instruct Pinnacle Law

Complete our simple online instruction form and your file is opened immediately. No upfront payment, no deposit — we get straight to work. We notify the other side’s solicitors and the estate agent that we are acting.

Instruction form File opened Parties notified
3

Complete Your Welcome Pack Tasks

We send you our Welcome Pack — a clear, guided checklist of everything we need from you to get the transaction underway. This includes identity verification, source of funds, and property information (for sellers). The sooner these are returned, the sooner we can proceed.

ID verification Source of funds Property information forms Mortgage details
4

Due Diligence — Contract, Searches & Enquiries

For buyers, we order and review all necessary searches and investigate the title to the property. For sellers, we prepare and send the contract pack to the buyer’s solicitors. We raise and respond to legal enquiries on your behalf throughout this stage.

Local authority searches Environmental searches Title investigation Contract pack Enquiries raised & answered
5

Reporting and Negotiation Stage

Once all searches and enquiries are complete, we report to you with a clear summary of everything we’ve found. We explain any issues in plain English, advise on next steps, and negotiate on your behalf where needed. You review, ask questions, and confirm you’re ready to proceed.

Report to client Mortgage offer review Issue resolution Client sign-off
6

Exchange of Contracts

Once all parties in the chain are ready, contracts are exchanged. This is the legally binding point — both buyer and seller are committed. A completion date is set and a deposit is transferred by the buyer. You can now make firm moving arrangements.

Final checks Deposit transferred Contracts exchanged Completion date fixed
7

Completion and Key Handover

On completion day we transfer the purchase funds, confirm completion with all parties, and authorise the release of the keys. The property is yours. We then handle all post-completion matters including Stamp Duty Land Tax submission and Land Registry registration.

Funds transferred Keys released Stamp duty submitted Land Registry registration

Frequently Asked Questions

Got questions? We've got answers.

How long does conveyancing take?
A typical transaction takes 8-12 weeks from instruction to completion, though this can vary depending on chain length, search turnaround times, and how quickly all parties respond. We work to keep things moving as fast as possible.
What's the difference between exchange and completion?
Exchange is when contracts become legally binding — both parties are committed. Completion is when money changes hands and you get the keys. There's usually 1-2 weeks between exchange and completion, though it can be on the same day.
What are searches and why do I need them?
Searches reveal important information about the property that isn't visible on a viewing — things like planning applications, flood risk, contaminated land, and local authority matters. Most mortgage lenders require them, and we recommend them even for cash buyers.
Who will be my point of contact?
Once we've reviewed your case, we'll pair you with a dedicated lawyer. You'll always have two named contacts — so if one is unavailable, the other can help immediately. Same-day responses are our standard.
