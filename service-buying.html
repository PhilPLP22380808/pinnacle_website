Other Costs to Consider

These are fees payable to third parties, not to Pinnacle:

Mortgage Broker Fees

If you use a mortgage broker, they may charge a fee for finding and arranging the right mortgage deal for you. Some brokers are paid purely by the lender, while others charge a flat fee or percentage. In many cases, a good broker can save you far more than they cost — but it's still something to budget for upfront so there are no surprises later.

Mortgage Arrangement Fees

Lender fees charged as part of your loan, including arrangement fees, booking fees, or survey/valuation fees. Sometimes these can be added to the mortgage rather than paid upfront.

Land Registry Registration Fees

The Government charge to process your purchase registration. This is paid to HM Land Registry, not your conveyancer. We'll help you know the value once your file is open.

Stamp Duty Land Tax

The transaction tax the Government charges on property transactions. This can be highly complex — use the Government calculator as a starting point.

Leasehold Admin Fees (if applicable)

If there's a management company, they may charge extra admin fees payable directly to them. Any work related to this is included in our quote — we don't add handling fees.

Indemnity Insurance (if needed)

You may decide to solve small or low-risk legal issues with insurance. We can arrange this at no extra cost other than the policy itself, payable on completion.

Removal Costs

Moving costs include everything involved in physically getting your belongings to your new home — a professional removals company, packing materials, storage, or additional charges such as parking restrictions or congestion zones. These can vary widely but always arrive right at the end of the process, so it's wise to plan ahead.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this really a fixed fee quote?

Yes. Our fee is a fully comprehensive, fixed and guaranteed price for our service. It never changes, even if complexities in the work are identified at a later date, which increases the amount of work required. We never charge you any more than we have quoted you here, upfront.

Other companies claim to offer a 'fixed fee' too — how is your quote different?

A common pricing structure in conveyancing is to offer a basic 'base' conveyancing legal fee, which is 'fixed', then increase the overall price with 'add-ons' depending on what applies to the transaction. The quote can claim to be 'fixed' but is increased far beyond the basic price with extra charges. For you as a client, it is important to know exactly what you are paying and whom you are paying it to. If your 'fixed legal fee' is £500 plus VAT, but you end up paying the law firm 3x or 4x that in total, is it really fair to call this price 'fixed'?

Do you take any upfront payments or deposit payments?

No. There is no upfront cost to you, and no deposit is required. Once you have completed our instruction form, we will simply get to work. Payment of our fees is only made if your transaction is successful and legal completion is achieved. You will be asked to pay our invoice just before you pick up your keys.

What happens if my purchase falls through? Do I still need to pay you?

No, we do not charge abortive costs. If your transaction is not successful for any reason — be that your decision or the decision of a third party — we do not charge you any abortive costs at all. If you do not purchase this property, you will likely look to purchase another one soon, and we would like to keep you as a client for your next purchase.

I've found a cheaper quote. What is the other quote missing to make it cheaper?

In short — it is probably not that much cheaper! Very cheap quotes often misrepresent the total costs by showing a cheap base fee but making it up with add-on charges either within the quote (with different names so it is unclear they are legal fees) or by adding charges hidden within the T&Cs or not disclosed at all. There is a functional minimum that all conveyancers have to charge. Do you want to trust such an important purchase with a firm that cannot disclose true costs upfront, and is a few hundred pounds really worth the trade-off in lost time, stress, and lack of support?

I've found much more expensive quotes. Why is yours so much cheaper?

A leading cause of higher conveyancing costs is referral fees or kickbacks to third parties that clients unknowingly subsidise. These referral fees can be many hundreds of pounds — up to half of the actual legal fee. If a business is giving away half its fee from day one, it must naturally charge much more to cover its costs. Pinnacle is an independent law firm and we never pass referral fees onto clients; we have a strict cap on the amount we pay for introduction and marketing costs.

Do you pay referral fees?

We pay referral fees as part of our marketing budget and have stringent internal guidelines on what is considered reasonable. As an independent law firm, we have refused to work with many industry partners — such as estate agents and mortgage brokers — because they demand referral fees above our cap. Most importantly, we never pass referral costs back to clients, and our fees remain fixed, so you know you are always getting a fair deal.

What are the next steps once I instruct you?

Once you have completed our instruction form and we have opened your file, we will send you our Welcome Pack which includes a list of tasks for us to work through together. We will also contact any third parties needed to move the process forward — such as the estate agent, your mortgage broker, and lender.

Who will I work with?

Once we have more information about your case, we will pair you with a lawyer best suited to your particular circumstances. You will always have two dedicated, named persons to contact — at any point either one will be able to assist you, so you always have an immediate point of contact.

Do you have capacity to handle my case?

We carefully monitor the volume of new clients we take on each month and have strict caps on how many clients we work with at one time. If we have reached our cap, we stop sending out quotes whilst we process existing work. If you are reading this, it means we are currently taking on new clients.

Do you have the expertise to handle my case?

We are a specialist CLC-regulated law firm and conveyancing is all we do — we are absolute experts in this space. As a group, we have collectively processed tens of thousands of transactions and have over 120 years of combined experience. You will have dedicated lawyers on your matter, but you also benefit from the knowledge of the whole firm. It is not likely that your purchase has any elements we have not seen before.
